Example 1: Problem Statement
Before: "Conventional wisdom says scaling parallelism always improves efficiency.

        they diverge dramatically depending on what is bottlenecking your job."

After:  "Here's the thing that most schedulers don't account for: scaling to
        more nodes doesn't always save energy, even when it saves time. The
        reason is that different jobs hit different bottlenecks."

↳ Effect: Personal observation instead of formal statement

Example 2: Case Study
Before: "Energy drops steeply until 8 nodes, then plateaus. At 64 nodes, energy
        per step is approximately 3x worse than the optimal point at 8 nodes."

After:  "Energy drops fast until you hit 8 nodes. Then it stops dropping. At
        64 nodes, energy is actually about 3x worse than at the 8-node sweet
        spot. Why? Each GPU node has fixed memory bandwidth."

↳ Effect: Human explanation instead of technical observation

Example 3: Limitations
Before: "Measurements are from one 64-node GPU cluster with NVIDIA A100 GPUs
        and Clos topology. Different network topologies may shift optimal node
        counts by 20-40%."

After:  "All measurements are from one 64-node cluster with A100 GPUs and Clos
        topology. If you have a different topology (dragonfly, fat-tree),
        different interconnects (InfiniBand vs. Ethernet), or more/fewer GPUs,
        optimal node counts could shift 20–40%."

↳ Effect: Conversational assessment of scope
